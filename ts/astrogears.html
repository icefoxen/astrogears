Astrogears

What is this?

This is a calculator to find gear ratios for a train of wheel-and-pinion gears. You give it the gear ratio you want as a decimal number, and it will find all sets of wheel-and-pinion gear trains that will give you that ratio to within a tolerance you specify.

How do I use this?

Enter your target gear ratio and max tolerance, and define the space for how many gear teeth you want for the wheels and pinions. "Length of gear train" is how many wheel-and-pinion sets you want.

How long does it take to calculate?

Try it and find out! Though start with small numbers and work your way up!!! I've put a fair amount of work into making the program run efficiently, but the number of potential solutions explodes the longer the gear train is. It has to do n2m calculations or something like that, where n is the number of gear teeth and m is the length of the gear train. On my computer, if you are looking for wheels and pinions with between 5 and 25 teeth, it goes through gear trains of length 2 in 0.1 second, gear trains of length 3 in 0.3 seconds, gear trains of length 4 in 11 seconds, and gear trains of length 5 in 200 seconds.

Needless to say, this is why the max gear train length is limited to 5. More than that and it's not terribly practical. Using a more powerful computer will help, but it's fairly easy to make situations that will take hours or days to run, at which point you'd be making your browser hang for minutes or hours at a time, and would gain a lot by having a more .
